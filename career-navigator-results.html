Where You Stand
Understanding Your Career Challenge

You're seeking to transition from senior technical leadership into a formal director or program manager role — moving from informal influence to institutional authority. The primary challenge isn't your qualifications: it's that your leadership work has happened inside a research identity, making it less legible to hiring decision-makers than candidates who've been on an explicit administrative track. The key is strategic repositioning, not credential-building.

Your Competitive Advantages
01
Rare Technical Credibility
A Ph.D. and 12 years of operational research gives you a credibility baseline that pure administrators can't match. Senior scientists respect this — it's your most durable leadership asset.
02
Proven Team Development at Scale
Four years leading an 8-person research team is substantial experience. The gap isn't what you've done — it's visibility. Your leadership work needs to be legible to decision-makers.
03
Cross-Agency Trust Networks
Your collaborative project history means you have internal champions across agencies. Director roles are rarely filled on paper credentials alone — internal endorsements are decisive.
04
Science-to-Operations Translation
Your ability to move research into operational products is increasingly what program directors are hired to do — you've been doing it at the individual contributor level, which is exactly the proof point leadership roles require.
Career Pathways

I've identified three pathways that align with your background, strengths, and career question. Each offers a distinct route forward — review them together before reading the recommendation below.

Pathway A

Federal Program Manager
/ Divisional Director

Recommended

The most direct path, leveraging your existing track record and internal relationships. Federal science agencies — NOAA, NWS, USGS, NASA — regularly promote senior researchers into program management roles. Your profile is precisely what these positions call for: deep domain expertise plus demonstrated team leadership.

Target Roles
GS-14/15 Program Manager, Branch Chief, Division Director, Research Program Coordinator
Why It Fits
Internal track means your credibility is already established. Your cross-agency network gives you an unusual number of potential internal sponsors.
Timeline
6–18 months with deliberate positioning; SES CDP can create a structured 18-month pathway
Key Consideration
Federal hiring moves slowly. Running a parallel external search at month 6 creates productive leverage without burning bridges.
Immediate Actions
  • Apply to the SES Candidate Development Program — next cohort deadlines are typically May and September
  • Request a career conversation with your division chief or a director you respect within 30 days
  • Identify 2–3 acting or detail opportunities that would put you in a program-level seat this year
Pathway B

Research Institute or
University Center Director

Alternative

National centers and university-affiliated research programs actively recruit senior federal scientists who want to move into leadership without leaving the science ecosystem. These roles offer more agility than federal positions and stronger external visibility — publishing, grants, and community engagement become part of the job description.

Target Organizations
UCAR/NCAR, CIRES, CIMSS, JCSDA, NOAA cooperative institutes, university atmospheric science departments
Why It Fits
Research credentials plus leadership track record is the exact profile these centers seek. You maintain scientific identity while gaining institutional authority.
Immediate Actions
  • Monitor UCAR, CIRES, and CIMSS job boards — director searches typically run 6–9 months
  • Build visibility by contributing to community science planning documents (decadal surveys, NOAA program reviews)
Pathway C

Science-Policy Bridge
/ Advisory Leadership

Longer-term

Roles at the intersection of science and policy — OSTP, NOAA policy offices, interagency working groups, or science advisor positions — offer significant institutional influence. Publications alone won't get you there, but testimony, task force leadership, and congressional engagement will. Best pursued after establishing Pathway A credentials.

Target Roles
OSTP Fellow, NOAA Science Advisor, NAS committee leadership, interagency liaison positions
Build Toward It
A 2–3 year federal director stint positions you strongly for science-policy roles — pursue in parallel with Pathway A

Pursue Pathway A now — activate Pathway B as a parallel track at month 6

Your strongest asset right now is internal credibility that external candidates simply can't match. Don't wait for a vacancy — initiate a conversation with your division leadership this month.
